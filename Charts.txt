<p> This page gives an illustration of the processes that underlie dependent arrival of wind storms over the BeNeLux. For background and terminology I refer to my thesis: <i> Clustering over the BeNeLux: Modelling dependent wind storms</i>. The arrival of winter storms tends to follow a dependent pattern. For possible causes of dependency (Malier, 2005) the distinction is made  between:
<ul>
  <li><font color="#40e0d0">Secondary cyclogenesis</font></li>
  <li><font color="#a02040">The steering of storms by large scale pressure variables</font></li>
  <li><font color="#202040">Independent storms</font></li>
</ul>
On the right side of this page a cluster of storms can be selected and the description and animation of the storms are displayed after pressing "Confirm choice". The size of the circles is proportional (*7000 pixels) to the three hour wind speed maxima, within a 6 degree radius of the vorticity maxima.
</p>

function RecurringTimer(callback, delay) {
    var timerId, start, remaining = delay;

    this.pause = function() {
        window.clearTimeout(timerId);
        remaining -= new Date() - start;
    };

    var resume = function() {
        start = new Date();
        timerId = window.setTimeout(function() {
            remaining = delay;
            resume();
            callback();
        }, remaining);
    };
    
    this.resume = resume;

    this.resume();
};
